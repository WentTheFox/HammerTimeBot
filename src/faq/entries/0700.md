# The timestamps generated for my timezone (e.g. EST, AEST) are off by 1 hour, why is that?
Many timezones have a "standard" and a "daylight" variant, and which is observed at any given time in a region can change. For example, if you are generating a timezone in EST (Eastern Standard Time) while EDT (Eastern Daylight Time) is _actually_ what's observed by your region, the time will be off by an hour compared to using the timezone that is currently being used. The simplest way to get the correct timestamp is to make sure you are using the correct time zone for your region and the time of year. Some timezones have combined variants accessible on the site, e.g. `EST5EDT` which will use the correct offset based on what is currently observed, but not all timezones have such a variant.

**Generally it is recommended to use the "named" timezones that correspond to a continent and the name of a major city, e.g. `America/New_York` as these will always give you the correct time for that region regardless of daylight-savings.**

Also, make sure your system clock is properly set. If your location recently switched to/from daylight-savings or you travelled recently with a laptop/tablet make sure the device's time is accurate for your current location, as the local machine time is the basis for all calculations on the website. The bot is not affected by your local time as those timestamps are calculated on the server side.
